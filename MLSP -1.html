import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

from scipy.stats import uniform

The point here is to find the a,b values which helps in maximizing the likelihood of finding the observed 5 values. The probability of finding any value under a distribution is represented by the area under the curve. The area under the curve is usually calculated by integrating the f(x) with a,b as limits but in uniform distribution its just the area of the rectangle.

If 'a' is greater than minimum of observations, then the likelihood is 0 or If 'b' is lesser than the maximum of the observations, then the likelihood is 0. This is because in both the cases you'll have the observations lying outside [a,b] leading to probability 0. In both the cases denominator 'b-a' gets bigger reducing the area under the curve implying less likelihood when compared to b= max(data) a=min(data)

To maximize the likelihood of finding the data in the the 'a' value must be the smallest value in the data, 'b' should be the largest element in the data. a= 90.9 b= 95.8
